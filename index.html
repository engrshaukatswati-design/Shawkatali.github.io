About

Academic profile and present direction

Currently, I am working towards my Ph.D. degree in Control Science and Engineering at Jiangsu University. My doctoral research investigates advanced diagnostic techniques for incipient broken rotor bar faults in induction motors. I am particularly interested in sensorless and low-cost diagnostic frameworks that can improve reliability under weak fault signatures, spectral interference, and low-frequency operating conditions.

My broader interests include signal processing for electrical machines, practical monitoring methods for industrial systems, and intelligent diagnostic approaches that connect theory, experimental validation, and real-world implementation.

Moreover, I am also interested in Multi-agent systems and Smart grids.

Research Overview

Main themes and technical emphasis

Current Research Focus

My current work centers on reliable diagnosis of broken rotor bar faults in induction motors, especially under conditions where fault signatures are weak, masked, or strongly affected by supply-related spectral components.

1

Weak fault signature extraction

Improving incipient fault visibility under low load and low-frequency operation.

2

Computational efficiency

Designing low-cost methods suitable for practical monitoring and real deployment.

3

Reliability and confirmation

Reducing false positives through slip confirmation and dual-signature consistency.

Signal Processing Methods

Envelope analysis, orthogonal-axis rectification, Goertzel estimation, adaptive PCA, and fault-sensitive component extraction (PCIP- Principal Component Inspired Projection).

Application Goal

Robust, sensorless, and experimentally validated diagnostic methods for induction motors in realistic working conditions.

Broader Direction

Intelligent diagnostics for rotating electrical machines and practical condition monitoring in industrial environments.

Selected Publications

Scholarly work and recent paper pipeline

Orthogonal Axis Rectification and Goertzel Analysis for Reliable Detection of Broken Rotor Bar Faults in Induction Motor
S. Ali, X. Song, W. Zhao, and H. Fan • Chinese Journal of Electrical Engineering • Accepted, 2026

This work develops a reliable diagnostic framework for broken rotor bar detection by combining orthogonal-axis rectification with Goertzel analysis, especially for low-load and low-frequency operation.

A Dual-Signature Rotor Fault Diagnosis Method Using Envelope Modulation and Slip Confirmation
S. Ali, X. Song, and W. Zhao • Chinese Journal of Electrical Engineering • Accepted, 2026

This paper proposes a dual-confirmation strategy using envelope modulation and slip-based consistency checking to strengthen diagnostic reliability and reduce false positives.

A Novel Cost-Effective Fault Diagnosis Method for Incipient Detection of Broken Rotor Bar in Induction Motor
S. Ali, X. Song, and W. Zhao • IEEJ Transactions on Electrical and Electronic Engineering • Under review, 2026

This study presents a low-cost incipient rotor fault diagnosis framework using adaptive principal component analysis and Goertzel-based estimation, together with a sensorless confirmation mechanism.

A practical approach to consensus based control of multi-agent systems
S. Ali, S. Ahmed, and S. N. Khan Marwat • 2018 International Symposium on Recent Advances in Electrical Engineering

An earlier contribution on consensus-based control for multi-agent systems, addressing coordination and power mismatch issues in distributed control settings.
