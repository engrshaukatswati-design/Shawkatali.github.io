About

Academic profile and current focus

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Control Science and Engineering at Jiangsu University. My doctoral work focuses on advanced diagnostic techniques for incipient broken rotor bar faults in induction motors. My broader research interest lies in intelligent diagnostics for rotating electrical machines, especially sensorless and computationally efficient approaches based on current signatures and signal processing.

Alongside research, I have experience in teaching, embedded and computer systems, telecommunications support, and interdisciplinary engineering applications. I am interested in contributing to high-quality research in intelligent diagnostics, electrical machines, signal processing, and engineering systems that connect theory with deployable practice.

Research Overview

Themes, methods, and applied direction

Induction Motor Fault Diagnosis

Development of reliable methods for early detection of broken rotor bar faults under weak-signature conditions, including incipient faults, low load, and challenging spectral environments.

Signal Processing Methods

Use of envelope analysis, Goertzel-based spectral estimation, orthogonal-axis rectification, APCA, and dual-signature confirmation strategies to improve diagnostic confidence and reduce false positives.

Practical Engineering Deployment

Emphasis on low-cost, sensorless, and computationally efficient diagnostic frameworks suitable for laboratory validation, industrial monitoring, and future intelligent maintenance systems.

Selected Publications

Accepted, under-review, and prior scholarly work

Orthogonal Axis Rectification and Goertzel Analysis for Reliable Detection of Broken Rotor Bar Faults in Induction Motor
S. Ali, X. Song, W. Zhao, and H. Fan • Chinese Journal of Electrical Engineering • Accepted, 2026

Presents a reliable diagnostic framework for broken rotor bar detection using orthogonal-axis rectification and Goertzel analysis, with strong performance under low-load and low-frequency operating conditions.

A Dual-Signature Rotor Fault Diagnosis Method Using Envelope Modulation and Slip Confirmation
S. Ali, X. Song, and W. Zhao • Chinese Journal of Electrical Engineering • Accepted, 2026

Proposes a dual-confirmation strategy that combines envelope modulation and slip-based consistency checking to enhance the reliability of broken rotor bar diagnosis.

A Novel Cost-Effective Fault Diagnosis Method for Incipient Detection of Broken Rotor Bar in Induction Motor
S. Ali, X. Song, and W. Zhao • IEEJ Transactions on Electrical and Electronic Engineering • Under review, 2026

Introduces a low-cost framework using adaptive principal component analysis and Goertzel-based estimation for incipient rotor bar fault detection, with a sensorless confirmation mechanism based on rotor-speed-related frequency estimation.

A practical approach to consensus based control of multi-agent systems
S. Ali, S. Ahmed, and S. N. Khan Marwat • 2018 International Symposium on Recent Advances in Electrical Engineering (RAEE)

Earlier work on distributed consensus control, addressing coordination and power mismatch problems in multi-agent systems.
